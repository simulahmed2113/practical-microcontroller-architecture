# MCU Study Plan

This file maps the MCU ecosystem that will be covered in this project. Not every family will be studied at the same depth. The goal is to balance deep understanding, practical value, and broad ecosystem awareness.

## Tier 1 — Deep Study
These families will receive detailed study and major book coverage.

1. AVR
2. STM32 / ARM Cortex-M
3. ESP32
4. PIC
5. RP2040

## Tier 2 — Medium Coverage / Comparison Study
These families are important for professional awareness and comparison.

6. MSP430
7. Nordic nRF Series
8. NXP LPC Series
9. NXP i.MX RT MCU Line
10. Renesas RA / RX / RL78
11. Infineon XMC / PSoC
12. Silicon Labs EFM8 / EFM32
13. TI Tiva / C2000
14. Microchip SAM Series

## Tier 3 — Low-Cost / Chinese MCU Ecosystem
These should be covered to reflect the real supply market, cost-sensitive design space, and practical engineering alternatives.

15. WCH CH32 Series
16. STC 8051 Family
17. Padauk
18. Nuvoton
19. GigaDevice GD32
20. MindMotion MM32
21. HC32
22. Puya and similar low-cost parts
23. Bouffalo / WinnerMicro / similar wireless-focused low-cost MCUs

## Tier 4 — High-Performance / Advanced Coverage
These are important for advanced engineering and later-stage study.

24. Cortex-M4 / M7 / M33 families
25. Teensy-class high-performance MCUs
26. Advanced motor-control MCU families
27. Automotive and safety-oriented MCU families
28. Dual-core and crossover MCU lines

## Recommended Learning Order
1. AVR
2. STM32
3. ESP32
4. PIC
5. RP2040
6. Broader ecosystem comparison
7. Cheap low-cost MCU landscape
8. Advanced high-performance MCU landscape

## Why This Order
- AVR builds low-level clarity
- STM32 builds modern 32-bit architecture understanding
- ESP32 adds wireless and software complexity
- PIC and RP2040 broaden architectural perspective
- Later families build market awareness and selection ability
